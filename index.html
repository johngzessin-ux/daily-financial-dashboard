Daily Financial Trends

July 13, 2026 · Morning research digest for FA conversations today

1. Private Investments

Private credit's redemption wave keeps $14B trapped. The $1.8T private credit market is facing its toughest stretch since 2008 — redemption requests are outpacing payouts, and fund managers don't expect the pressure to ease quickly. Worth flagging to clients holding direct-lending allocations who may be feeling liquidity anxiety.
Source: Bloomberg — "Private Credit Funds Trap $14 Billion as Redemption Requests Outpace Payouts" (July 2, 2026)
Yet allocators aren't backing off. Over 80% of portfolio managers still expect increased private credit allocations over the next 12 months, with capital rotating toward asset-backed finance and non-sponsor lending as the "safer" corners of the market.
Source: PwC — Private Credit Survey 2026
PE-owned life insurers are leaning further into alternative credit. Apollo- and KKR-owned insurers have quietly reshaped portfolios toward higher-yielding alternative credit — a read on how deep the institutional appetite for private credit really runs, even amid the redemption headlines above.
Source: Bloomberg — "PE-Owned Insurers Boosting Private Credit Holdings" (May 13, 2026)

2. Equities

Futures slid Monday as the Iran conflict escalated. S&P 500 futures fell ~0.3% and Nasdaq 100 futures dropped 0.8% after the US renewed strikes near the Strait of Hormuz over the weekend and Iran retaliated against Kuwait, Jordan, and Qatar. Brent crude surged toward $80/barrel.
Source: Yahoo Finance — "Stock market today" (July 13, 2026)
Q2 earnings season kicks off this week. Banks report starting July 14, big tech follows the week of July 20. FactSet is forecasting 22% S&P 500 earnings growth for Q2 — the second straight quarter above 20%, a strong offset to the geopolitical noise.
Source: TheStreet Pro — "Strong Rotation Prevents Broad Weakness"
SpaceX's Nasdaq-100 bump may not stick. SpaceX joined the index July 7, just 15 trading days post-IPO, with JPMorgan estimating $4.3B of index-driven buying. But a staggered lockup expiration starting around mid-July earnings releases ~20% of pre-IPO shares — history suggests these post-inclusion pops tend to fade.
Source: The Motley Fool (July 6, 2026)
Ben Carlson's counterpoint to the bearish headlines. His latest notes earnings growth is actually accelerating even as bearish narratives dominate — a useful talking point for clients who are hearing only the scary version of the story.
Source: A Wealth of Common Sense — "10 Reasons to be Bearish" (July 10, 2026)

3. Fixed Income

10-year yield hit a ~2-month high. The 10-year Treasury climbed to 4.58% on July 13 after rising ~10bps over the prior two sessions; 2-year sits at 4.21%, 30-year at 5.06%.
Source: Advisor Perspectives (dshort) — "Treasury Yields Snapshot"
Fed expected to hold July 28-29, but September odds are climbing. Markets currently price ~64% odds of a hike by the September meeting as inflation stays sticky; effective fed funds rate is 3.63%.
Source: Federal Reserve — FOMC Statement
Fed leadership signaling a framework review. NY Fed's John Williams flagged AI-driven demand as his top inflation watch item, while Fed Chair Kevin Warsh has launched five task forces reviewing the Fed's policy approach — worth watching for clients curious whether the Fed's playbook itself is changing.
Source: Federal Reserve — H.15 Selected Interest Rates
Tomorrow's CPI print is a big swing factor. June CPI lands Tuesday July 14 at 8:30am ET — with oil spiking on the Iran conflict, this print carries extra weight for the Fed's rate path.
Source: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics — CPI Summary

4. Geopolitical Events

US-Iran conflict escalates near the Strait of Hormuz. The US struck near the strait over the weekend; Iran retaliated against Kuwait, Jordan, and Qatar. Iran's IRGC claims the strait is closed "until further notice," which the US disputes — but vessel crossings dropped to just 6 in one recent 12-hour window vs. the normal 18-22/day.
Source: Al Jazeera — "Oil prices jump as US and Iran trade attacks over Strait of Hormuz" (July 13, 2026)
Bloomberg — "Oil Supply Adapts to Hormuz Disruptions": Bloomberg Intelligence's Mike McGlone on markets largely accepting US assurances the strait remains open
Brent crude jumped over 4% Monday. The strait carries roughly 20% of global petroleum liquids consumption (~20M barrels/day in 2024) — a genuine chokepoint risk if disruption drags on, and a direct line into the CPI print above.
Source: U.S. Energy Information Administration
Tariff deadlines are stacking up. Section 122 balance-of-payments tariffs (10%) expire July 24; separately, new 50% tariffs on copper and on Brazilian exports take effect August 1, while the just-locked Japan trade deal caps auto tariffs at 15% (well below the 25% previously floated).
Source: Investing.com (Marc Chandler) — "July Market Outlook"
China's rare-earth curbs on Japan are drawing direct US intervention. Washington is asking Beijing to ease restrictions — a live risk factor for any client conversation touching semiconductors, defense, or EV supply chains.
Source: McKinsey — "Geopolitics and the Geometry of Global Trade: 2026 Update"
