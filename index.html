July 17, 2026 · Morning research digest for FA conversations today

1. Private Investments

Cliffwater's $31B private credit fund caps redemptions again. Investors sought to pull ~17% of shares in Q2 (up from 14% in Q1); Cliffwater capped payouts at 5% (down from 7% last quarter), meaning investors get back less than 30 cents per dollar requested. S&P Global lowered its outlook on the fund to negative, calling the 5% threshold "an important guardrail." A concrete data point for clients holding interval-fund private credit who are asking about liquidity.
Source: Bloomberg — "Cliffwater Private Credit Fund Stung by 17% Redemption Requests"
It's part of a broader pattern. The $1.8T private credit market's redemption wave is showing no signs of abating as firms report Q2 results — this isn't an isolated Cliffwater story.
Source: Advisor Perspectives
PE is being reframed as a "mature industry." McKinsey's latest Global Private Markets Report argues the conditions that once amplified PE returns — falling rates, expanding multiples, abundant leverage — have passed. Useful context for clients wondering why PE returns feel less spectacular than a few years ago.
Source: McKinsey — Global Private Markets Report 2026

2. Equities

Chip selloff dragged the market down Thursday despite solid earnings. The S&P 500 fell 0.51% to 7,533.77 and the Nasdaq Composite dropped 1.47% to 25,881.95, triggered by TSMC raising its capex guidance to $60-64B (from $52-56B) — spooking investors about AI-spending sustainability even though TSMC's own quarter beat estimates.
Source: CNBC — Stock Market Today
But breadth stayed healthy. 8 of 11 S&P sectors were actually higher Thursday despite the headline decline, and 62% of S&P 500 stocks trade above their 50-day moving average — worth mentioning to clients who see a red index and assume a broad selloff.
The TSMC story in more depth. Bloomberg breaks down TSMC's results and its bigger US buildout commitment — the "bad" reaction was about spending sustainability, not fundamentals.
Bloomberg Tech — "TSMC's Market Jolt, Saronic's Texas Bet and GameStop's eBay Bid" (July 16, 2026)
Ben Carlson: "The Cult of Equities." Examines whether today's high household equity allocations signal overvaluation or are simply the new normal — good framing for clients worried they're "too heavy" in stocks.
Source: A Wealth of Common Sense (July 14, 2026)

3. Fixed Income

10-year yield pushing back toward recent highs. The 10-year Treasury rose to 4.60% Thursday, approaching the ~2-month high of 4.62% hit July 13, as markets continue positioning for a possible Fed hike later this year.
Source: U.S. Department of the Treasury — Daily Treasury Rates
Full curve as of July 16 close: 3-month 3.79%, 1-yr 3.98%, 2-yr 4.16%, 5-yr 4.28%, 10-yr 4.57%, 30-yr 5.09%. The 10s-3mo spread is +78bp and 10s-2s is +41bp — still positively sloped, consistent with a cautious-growth-plus-persistent-inflation read.
Source: StreetStats — U.S. Treasury Yield Curve
Fed funds futures still leaning toward a hike, not a cut. Futures price a path rising toward ~3.8% by October and near 4% by year-end; the July 28-29 meeting is expected to hold steady.
Source: StreetStats — Fed Funds Rate Forecast
Ben Carlson: "Do We Need the Federal Reserve?" Timely listen for clients asking bigger-picture questions about Fed independence, given the ongoing Warsh-era policy review.
Source: A Wealth of Common Sense — Talk Your Book (July 13, 2026)

4. Geopolitical Events

US reimposed a naval blockade on Iranian shipping through the Strait of Hormuz and now demands payment for all other cargo transiting the waterway. Oil held its biggest gain since April on the news.
Source: Bloomberg — Oil Market News (July 13, 2026)
Iran is going dark to move oil anyway. Six US-sanctioned supertankers (12M combined barrels capacity) transited the strait with transponders switched off in the past week, and Iran reportedly exported 57M barrels of crude during a pause between blockades — the blockade's real-world bite looks limited so far.
Source: Bloomberg — "Iran Exported 57 Million Barrels of Crude During US Blockade Pause"
Bloomberg opinion: Iran risks "overplaying its hand." Squeezing the strait too hard could cost Iran strategic leverage over the next five years as neighbors build bypass routes — even without immediate relief for markets.
Source: Bloomberg Opinion (July 16, 2026)
Contagion hit Europe too. Germany's DAX fell 2.76%, France's CAC 40 dropped 1.99%, Italy's FTSE MIB slipped 0.39% — and separately, the administration is prepping a new round of global tariffs that could add fresh upward pressure on H2 import prices.
Source: NPR
