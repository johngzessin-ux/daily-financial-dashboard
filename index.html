1. Private Investments

Private credit: "not in crisis, but facing its first major test." Managers are raising for expansion while deploying as though a downturn may already be underway — a split posture worth flagging to clients who ask whether the redemption headlines (Cliffwater, etc.) mean the whole asset class is in trouble.
Source: 0100 Weekly Brief — "Private Credit Under the Microscope: Fear, AI Risk and the Institutional View"
Yet the biggest players keep expanding aggressively. Apollo is reportedly targeting $300B of Wall-Street-style lending and a separate $20B target for private credit in Mexico — a sign conviction at the top of the market hasn't wavered even as smaller funds cap redemptions.
Source: Within Intelligence — "Private Credit Outlook 2026"
Ben Carlson digs into private credit's liquidity mismatch. A timely explainer for clients holding interval funds who are asking why they can't just "sell" like a normal bond fund.
Source: A Wealth of Common Sense — "Talk Your Book: Private Credit's Next Act" (July 20, 2026)

Source: Yahoo Finance — "Stock market today" (July 22, 2026)
Alphabet and Tesla report after today's close. A real test of whether AI-capex optimism can hold up after last week's chip-sector selloff — worth watching for clients with concentrated mega-cap tech exposure.
Source: Bloomberg — "S&P Futures Fall as Traders Brace for Alphabet, Tesla Earnings"
A new, longer-dated tariff wrinkle: generic drugs. Imported generics face a 100% tariff starting August 2028, stepping up to 200% a year later, aimed at reshoring production — a slow-burn story worth having in your back pocket for healthcare-sector conversations.
Source: Benzinga
